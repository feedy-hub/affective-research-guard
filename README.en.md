## 🌸 What is this Skill?

**Affective Research Guard** is a lightweight Skill for research-oriented conversations.  
It is designed for long discussions about ideas, papers, experiments, submission strategy, and technical roadmaps.

Think of it as a tiny research-conversation organizer:

> 🫶 Notice the human state before the discussion becomes messy.  
> 🔬 Then review ideas, evidence, experiments, and claims with rigorous standards.

---

## 🧸 What problems does it solve?

| Scenario | Common problem | What it helps with |
|---|---|---|
| 🌀 Messy thinking | Ideas, experiments, and paper story are mixed together | Reorganizes the discussion into goals, assumptions, evidence, risks, and next steps |
| 💡 Idea review | AI only says “this has potential” | Re-evaluates contribution, testability, and reviewer risk |
| 🧪 Experiment design | Many experiments, but none prove the core claim | Finds the smallest decisive validation path |
| 📝 Paper writing | The story sounds smooth, but the problem framing is weak | Checks problem definition, method necessity, and conclusion boundary |
| ⏰ Before submission | Only strengths are discussed | Previews likely reviewer concerns |

---

## 🧩 Core capabilities

| Module | Purpose |
|---|---|
| 🧭 State structuring | Turns messy discussion into a structure that can move forward |
| 🔍 Idea review | Checks whether the problem, contribution, and novelty are clear |
| 📏 Goal grounding | Converts vague goals into measurable, verifiable, and reviewable conditions |
| 🧪 Experiment calibration | Keeps experiments connected to the core claim |
| 🧑‍⚖️ Reviewer preview | Surfaces the most likely reviewer concerns before submission |

---

## 🍃 Before and after

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| “This idea feels good.” | “What are its contribution, risk, and minimal validation experiment?” |
| “Should I add more experiments?” | “Which experiment directly proves the core claim?” |
| “I feel confused now.” | “Let’s separate the goal, assumptions, evidence, and next step.” |
| “Can this target a top venue?” | “Let’s check problem framing, novelty boundary, baseline risk, and evidence strength.” |
